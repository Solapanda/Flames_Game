How to Play FLAMES

FLAMES is a classic pen-and-paper love game that's been played by kids and teenagers for decades. The name stands for Friends, Lovers, Affection, Marriage, Enemies (Tension), and Siblings (BFFs).

The Rules

  1. Write out both names and remove all common letters from each
  2. Count the total remaining letters — this is your magic number
  3. Use that number to count through F-L-A-M-E-S, eliminating one letter each round
  4. The last letter standing reveals your relationship destiny
Frequently Asked Questions

Is FLAMES accurate? FLAMES is a fun game for entertainment — it uses a simple letter-counting algorithm and isn't based on any science. Enjoy it for what it is!

Can I play with any names? Yes! Try it with friends, crushes, celebrities, fictional characters — anyone you're curious about.

Why did I get Tension? Tension (the E in FLAMES) means there's electric, passionate energy between the two names. Many great love stories start with a little tension!
